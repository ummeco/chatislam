What are the Five Pillars of Islam?
The Five Pillars of Islam are the core acts of worship that every Muslim is obligated to perform. They are: (1) the Shahada — declaration of faith that there is no god but Allah and Muhammad is His messenger; (2) Salah — five daily prayers at prescribed times; (3) Zakat — obligatory charity given annually; (4) Sawm — fasting during the month of Ramadan; and (5) Hajj — pilgrimage to Mecca at least once in a lifetime for those who are able.
Quran 2:177 · Sahih al-Bukhari 8
Can non-Muslims visit a mosque?
Yes — non-Muslims are generally welcome to visit mosques in most parts of the world, particularly for educational and interfaith purposes. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ received delegations from non-Muslim tribes in the mosque of Madinah.
Sahih Muslim 1764 · IslamQA.info

Is Islam a religion of peace?
ChatIslam
Islam — from the Arabic root "salm" meaning peace — teaches that peace is central to the faith. The Quran states: "O you who believe, enter into peace wholeheartedly" (2:208). Islam emphasizes peaceful coexistence and prohibits aggression. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said: "The Muslim is the one from whose tongue and hand the people are safe" (Sahih al-Bukhari 10). Like any major religion, the nuances of its history and theology are rich and scholarly — but the foundational texts are consistent in upholding peace, justice, and mercy.
Quran 2:208 · Sahih al-Bukhari 10

What do Muslims believe about Jesus (Isa)?
ChatIslam
Muslims hold Jesus (known as Isa in Arabic) in very high regard — he is one of the five greatest prophets in Islam (the Ulul Azm). Muslims believe he was born of a virgin (Maryam), performed miracles by God's permission, and was raised to the heavens without dying on the cross. The Quran dedicates an entire chapter (Surah Maryam, Chapter 19) to his mother and his birth. Muslims do not believe Jesus is the Son of God or part of a Trinity — they affirm he was a human prophet and the Messiah, but not divine.
Quran 3:45-55 · Surah Maryam 19:16-35

What is Ramadan and why do Muslims fast?
ChatIslam
Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar, during which Muslims fast from dawn (Fajr) until sunset (Maghrib) — abstaining from food, drink, and other physical needs. Fasting is the fourth Pillar of Islam and is obligatory for every adult Muslim who is physically able. Allah says in the Quran: "O you who believe, fasting is prescribed for you as it was prescribed for those before you, that you may attain taqwa (God-consciousness)" (2:183). Beyond hunger, Ramadan is a month of heightened worship, Quran recitation, charity, and community.
Quran 2:183-185 · Sahih al-Bukhari 1901
